AEOLIAN DAY

Presenting Bay Area instrument inventors’ collaborations with a force of nature, Aeolian Day is a recurring festival in Oakland, CA, mounted by Thingamajigs. Aeolian Day exhibits solo works and installations created with local OUSD schoolchildren. The event fosters the creative interplay of art, music, and science through innovative sound sculptures.

Aeolian Day is a blend of sound sculptures created by artists, teaching artists and their students, and workshop participants installed throughout an open space to create different pockets of music harnessed by the wind. Together professional and amateur makers of all age ranges get to showcase the creativity of Oakland, and the wonderful power of the wind.

SUDHU TEWARI

Sudhu Tewari has been described as a bricoleur, junkyard maven, and audio-gadgeteer. He invents and builds musical instruments, kinetic and sound sculpture, interactive installations, audio electronics, and wearable sound art. He holds a B.A. in Music from Sonoma State University, an M.F.A. in Electronic Music and Recording Technology from Mills College, and a Ph.D. in Cultural Musicology from UC Santa Cruz. Dr. Tewari is Workshop Technician in the instrument building program at Mills College, and teaches at California College of the Arts, the University of San Francisco, and Expression College.

THINGAMAJIGS 

In 1997, Edward Schocker and Dylan Bolles sat together at Mills College in Oakland, California thinking of ways to create a network of composers and performers to develop and nurture the exploration of alternative materials and methods of creating sounds as well as promote DIY and MAKER collaboration between artists and local communities. Through this desire, came about Thingamajigs. Our mission is to explore sounds in found objects and use alternative tuning systems to create new music. We are on a constant search of sounds yet to be found. Throughout our history, Thingamajigs has premiered new works and performances by over 275 local, national, and international artists. We offer an annual music festival, in-school and after-school classes, and individual concerts and community workshops throughout the year. Many of our artists have gained international recognition - with two MacArthur “Genius” Fellows, Gerbode Foundation's Emerging Composers Awardees, and a McKnight Composer Fellow.
