04/2021
Call for LGBTQ and/or BIPOC Artists for the Healing Garden

West End Arts District and West Alameda Business Assocaiation are seeking LGBTQ and/or BIPOC artists to paint original works to expand the exhibit at The Healing Garden.  The current works were created to voice our community support for the Black Lives Matter movement in the wake of the death of George Floyd. As the Healing Garden re-opens for the 2021 season, we are looking to add works that celebrate diversity and look to a brighter future in 2021 and beyond. We will provide each artist with one 8' x 4’ plywood board and a stipend of $500. We will notify selected artists by 04/28 and collect completed works for installation by 05/15/21.

02/2021
Meet the artist and help us introduce him to the West End community.

WEAD is hosting two zoom gatherings to introduce our community to the artist behind the future Webster Gateway Mural.  Dave Young Kim is an Oakland-based, fine-artist and muralist whose work is rooted in his Korean ancestry. Kim refers to himself as a 'community artist,'  working hand in hand with the community to create work that reflects and inspires them.  We'd love you to join us on zoom and share your stories and experiences of life in Alameda's West End.
